Legal Case Summarisation Project -- AIthena (Internship)

During my internship at AIthena, I worked on reproducing the Marro et al. paper by building a legal case summariser using Legal-BERT embeddings, multi-headed attention, BiLSTM, and CRF layers. The aim was to automatically label rhetorical roles in judgements and generate concise summaries, helping reduce the time required to review lengthy legal cases.

My contributions:

  • Data preparation: processed and cleaned large sets of case data in JSON format, building efficient preprocessing pipelines.
  • Model experimentation: trained and validated NLP models, tuning embeddings and model weights for performance.
  • Applied learning: gained hands-on experience with attention mechanisms, sequence modeling, and evaluation in a real-world legal tech context.
